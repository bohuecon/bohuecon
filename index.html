Welcome to Bo Hu's Pages

I am a PhD candidate in Economics at Vrije Universiteit Amsterdam and Tinbergen Institute. My research areas are labor economics and industrial organization. I am particularly interested in labor search and consumer search. I am under the supervision of Makoto Watanabe and Pieter Gautier. I expect to complete my PhD in September 2018. Here is my CV.

Research

Market-making middlemen, with Pieter Gautier and Makoto Watanabe, August 2016. [Paper] [Slides]
Why intermediaries are of different structures? For example, eBay is a pure platform while Amazon combines a platform and its inventory. How is this related to the market competition? This paper provides a theory to explain this pattern.

Competing Market-making Middlemen [Work in progress]
This project extends market-making middlemen by having two intermediaries competing on prices/fees. Under various announcement sequences, we show the same intuition carries through and a market-making middleman emerges endogenously.

Executive Compensation and Search Frictions [Work in progress]
Why executives/CEOs are compensated so differently among firms of different sizes? Other than traditional theories such as asymmetric information or rent extraction, is there an explanation that search frictions can contribute?
